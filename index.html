What's a computer?

A computer is a programmable electronic device that can store, retrieve, and process data. It is capable of performing complex calculations and logical operations, and it operates on data in binary form using hardware components such as a central processing unit (CPU), memory, and storage devices. Computers are used in a wide range of fields, including business, education, healthcare, science, and engineering. They can be programmed to perform a variety of tasks, from simple arithmetic calculations to advanced scientific simulations. Modern computers are highly interconnected, allowing for communication and sharing of data across networks and the internet.

Why is useful a computer?

Computers are useful because they can process large amounts of data quickly and accurately, perform complex calculations, automate repetitive tasks, connect people and devices across the world, store and organize vast amounts of information, and provide access to vast amounts of knowledge and entertainment. They are essential tools in many fields, including education, business, healthcare, science, and engineering.

Why is better to build a computer instead to buy one already built?

Building a computer yourself can have several advantages over buying a pre-built one. First, you have complete control over the selection of components, allowing you to customize the build to meet your specific needs and preferences. This can result in better performance, reliability, and cost-effectiveness. Second, building a computer can be a rewarding and educational experience, as you gain a better understanding of how the hardware components work together. Additionally, you have the ability to upgrade and modify the computer over time, extending its useful lifespan. Finally, building a computer can be a fun and engaging hobby for enthusiasts.
